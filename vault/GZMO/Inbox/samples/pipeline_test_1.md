What is the Cognitive Drive Architecture formula (Lagun's Law)? How does cortisol prevent sedation?



---

## GZMO Response
*2026-04-18T11:29:59.150Z*

Cognitive Drive Architecture (CDA) employs Lagun's Law, which posits that an agent's drive yield \(D\) is influenced by the balance of Anchory (\(A\)) and Primode (\(P\)) variables:

\[ D = A \times P^k \]

Where \( k \) is a constant factor.

Cortisol prevents sedation by dynamically modulating the Primode threshold. When Grain (an indicator of interoceptive stress) rises or Slip occurs, cortisol triggers an immediate response to force upregulation of the Anchory variable (\(A\)). This counteracts rising friction and ensures the Drive yield \(D\) remains above a minimum operational threshold, thereby preventing total dropout.

In essence, cortisol acts as a stabilizing agent that maintains cognitive activity by continuously adjusting the system's tension-bias against interoceptive stressors.
